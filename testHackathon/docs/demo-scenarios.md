# Demo Scenarios

## Overview

These scenarios are designed for effective hackathon demonstration. Each tells a coherent story that showcases the platform's capabilities.

---

## Scenario 1: The Slipping Initiative

### Context
The Ministry of Digital Economy has a critical e-services modernization initiative that is falling behind schedule.

### Demo Flow

**Step 1: Executive Radar**
- Open Executive Dashboard
- Notice "Digital Services Modernization" flagged RED
- See 23% behind schedule indicator
- Notice linked citizen complaints rising

**Step 2: Drill into Readiness**
- Click through to Institutional Readiness
- Ministry of Digital Economy shows:
  - Technology: 78/100 ✓
  - Capabilities: 45/100 ⚠️
  - Governance: 52/100 ⚠️
- Capability gap: "Lack of senior technical architects"

**Step 3: Check Coordination**
- Navigate to Cross-Entity view
- See dependency: Ministry depends on National Data Center
- Blocker: "Data center migration delayed by 6 weeks"
- Escalation status: Director level (day 12)

**Step 4: Chief of Staff Action**
- Open Chief of Staff Office
- Meeting prep already generated for tomorrow's review
- Suggested talking points include:
  - Address capability gap with secondment
  - Escalate data center blocker to ministerial level
- Create follow-up action item

### Key Insight Demonstrated
The platform connects initiative status → readiness gaps → cross-entity blockers → executive action in one coherent flow.

## Scenario 2: The Painful Service

### Context
Citizens are complaining heavily about business license renewal services.

### Demo Flow

**Step 1: Citizen Voice**
- Open Citizen Voice Translator
- See "Business License Renewal" as top recurring theme
- 847 complaints in last quarter
- Sentiment: 78% negative
- Trust pulse: declining trend

**Step 2: Service Friction Analysis**
- Navigate to Service Friction Intelligence
- Business License Renewal shows:
  - Friction Score: 82/100 (high pain)
  - Root causes:
    - 7 documents required (vs. 3 benchmark)
    - Manual verification step
    - Cross-ministry check creates 8-day delay
  - Category: PROCESS + HANDOFF

**Step 3: Quick Win Identification**
- View recommended actions:
  - Quick Win: Digital document upload (reduces 2 visits)
  - Medium Term: Automated verification via X system
  - Strategic: Unified business registry integration

**Step 4: Policy Consideration**
- Navigate to Policy Impact
- See policy option: "Mandate unified business registry"
- Impact assessment shows:
  - 12 ministries affected
  - Implementation: 18 months
  - Risk: legacy system migration
- Tradeoff: faster vs. comprehensive approach

### Key Insight Demonstrated
Citizen complaints drive friction analysis → actionable quick wins → policy considerations, all connected.

## Scenario 3: The Readiness Gap

### Context
A new public procurement reform is planned, but some entities may not be ready.

### Demo Flow

**Step 1: Readiness Analyzer**
- Open Institutional Readiness
- Filter by "Procurement Reform Readiness"
- See heatmap:
  - Ministry of Finance: 85/100 (Ready)
  - Ministry of Health: 62/100 (Needs Support)
  - Municipality of Amman: 71/100 (Partially Ready)
  - Social Security Corporation: 38/100 (Not Ready)

**Step 2: Gap Analysis**
- Click on Social Security Corporation
- Critical gaps:
  - No dedicated procurement unit (Governance: 32)
  - Legacy financial system (Technology: 41)
  - Limited reform experience (Culture: 35)
- Recommended actions: establish unit, plan system upgrade

**Step 3: Policy Impact Check**
- Navigate to Policy Impact
- See procurement reform analysis:
  - Option A: Phased rollout (12 entities, then expand)
  - Option B: Full rollout (all 48 entities)
- System recommends Option A due to readiness gaps
- Risk note: "4 entities score below 50; mandatory support needed"

**Step 4: Executive Brief**
- Return to Executive Radar
- See "Procurement Reform" item requiring decision
- Recommended action: "Approve phased approach with support package"

### Key Insight Demonstrated
Readiness data directly informs policy decisions and reform sequencing.

## Scenario 4: The Ministerial Meeting

### Context
Minister has a modernization steering committee meeting tomorrow.

### Demo Flow

**Step 1: Chief of Staff Office**
- Open Chief of Staff view
- See tomorrow's meeting: "Modernization Steering Committee"
- Meeting prep pack auto-generated

**Step 2: Meeting Prep Content**
- View prep pack:
  - Attendees: 8 secretary generals
  - Agenda: 4 items
  - For each agenda item:
    - Background context
    - Current status
    - Key data points
    - Discussion questions
    - Recommended positions

**Step 3: Link to Other Capabilities**
- Agenda item: "Digital Services Update"
  - Links to Executive Radar item
  - Shows behind-schedule status
- Agenda item: "Citizen Feedback Report"
  - Links to Citizen Voice summary
  - Top 3 themes pre-loaded

**Step 4: Post-Meeting**
- After meeting, record decisions:
  - "Approve emergency capability support for SSC"
  - "Escalate data center delay to Cabinet"
- Create follow-ups with owners and deadlines
- Track completion status

### Key Insight Demonstrated
Chief of Staff Office integrates all other capabilities into actionable executive support.

## Scenario 5: The Cross-Entity Block

### Context
A shared service platform is blocked by coordination failure.

### Demo Flow

**Step 1: Cross-Entity Engine**
- Open Coordination Engine
- View "Shared Services Platform" initiative
- Dependency map shows:
  - Ministry of ICT (lead) → Ministry of Finance (budget)
  - Ministry of ICT → Civil Service Bureau (HR integration)
  - Civil Service Bureau → National Registry (data)

**Step 2: Identify Blocker**
- Blocker flagged: "Civil Service Bureau HR API"
- Status: Overdue by 18 days
- Escalation: Secretary General level (14+ days)
- Block reason: "Security review pending"

**Step 3: Impact Analysis**
- See downstream impact:
  - Ministry of ICT blocked
  - 3 other ministries waiting
  - Total delay: estimated 6 weeks

**Step 4: Resolution Path**
- Navigate to Chief of Staff
- See escalation in queue
- Create ministerial intervention request
- Track resolution through coordination dashboard

### Key Insight Demonstrated
Dependency tracking identifies coordination failures before they cascade.
