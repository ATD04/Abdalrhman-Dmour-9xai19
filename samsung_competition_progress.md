## What the competition is

I'm making a short video (30–45 seconds) using **only AI** — no real filming. The goal is to show how technology fits into life in a magical, cinematic way. I picked **Track B**: showing how AI can "complete the moment" (restore/transform an image). The deadline is **21 June 2026 at 4 PM**, and the prize for 1st place is $2,000 + a Galaxy S26 Ultra + a freelance contract.

## My story idea

A cute fennec fox finds an old, torn, faded photo in the desert. It pushes the photo toward a glowing phone, the phone's AI "completes" the photo, and the picture comes back to life — colors return and it blooms into a living memory around the fox. I chose a fox because the competition rewards cute animals, and it has no human faces (which the rules prefer).

## My plan: 7 shots

1. Fox digs in the sand at dusk
2. Fox uncovers the old torn photo
3. Fox nudges the photo toward a glowing phone
4. A beam of light scans the photo (the magic moment)
5. The photo restores itself — colors flood back (the WOW moment)
6. The photo blooms into a living memory around the fox
7. The finished glowing photo on the phone, fox happy (the ending)

## What I did today, step by step

### 1. Set up my account
I subscribed to the Google AI Plus plan so I could use the AI tools.

### 2. Made my fox character ("the character bible")
In Gemini, I generated my fox from 4 angles, all matching the same look (same fur, ears, eyes, golden desert mood):

- Side view

I downloaded all 4 and saved them in a folder called `1_References`.

### 3. Built the fox as a "Character" in Flow
In Flow, I uploaded all 4 fox images and made a saved Character named **Fennec**. This is the trick that keeps the fox looking the same in every video clip.

### 4. Set up my Flow settings
- Confirm before generating: **Always** (so I don't waste credits by accident)
- Video size: **16:9** (I'll make the vertical 9:16 version later in editing)
- Count: **1x** (one clip at a time, to save credits)
- Model: **Veo 3.1 - Fast** (good quality, cheaper than Quality, so I can do more tries)

### 5. Started generating video shots
For each shot I type `@Fennec` plus a description of the action, then add this style line to the end every time:
> Cinematic, photorealistic, warm golden-hour desert lighting, shallow depth of field, soft natural motion, premium quality, no text or logos.

## Things I learned today

- Always feed the fox into the shot (`@Fennec`) so it stays consistent. Never describe the fox from scratch again.
- Flow keeps every version in the **history strip** at the top, so I can never lose a clip — I can always go back to the original.
- Tell the AI what I DON'T want too (like "no big sand clouds"), not just what I want.
- Keep each shot to one simple action. Complex actions glitch.
- Don't name "Samsung" or "Galaxy" in the prompt — the AI can't draw real products well. Show the feeling instead.
- Don't over-spend credits perfecting small shots. Save tries for the wow shots (5 and 6).
