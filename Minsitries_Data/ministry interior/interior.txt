1. 0003_ميثاق_تقديم_الخدمة.pdf (Service Delivery Charter)
Why it is the best: This document is the definitive guide to the citizen-Ministry relationship. It tells the LLM exactly how the Ministry classifies its users, what the expected standard of service is, and the mutual rights and responsibilities of both parties.

Core Use Cases:

General Inquiry Handling: Answering questions like "What documents do I need to prepare before visiting?" or "What are the rules for entering the Ministry building?"

Rights & Duties: Informing users of their rights regarding data privacy, expected wait times, and the facilities available to them (like accessibility for the elderly).

Setting Expectations: Explaining what happens if a user submits an incomplete application according to the official charter.

2. 0005_دليل_الشكاوى.pdf (Complaints Guide 2025)
Why it is the best: A chatbot’s most valuable function is often troubleshooting. This document gives the LLM the exact, up-to-date (2025) operational workflow for handling grievances. Instead of giving vague advice, the LLM can provide real phone numbers, emails, and procedural steps.

Core Use Cases:

Issue Resolution Routing: Directing frustrated users on exactly how to file a complaint if a service is delayed or if they encounter an administrative error.

Contact Information: Providing the LLM with official, verified contact channels, such as the Inspector General's email, the official WhatsApp complaint number, and instructions for the "Bukhadmatkum" (At Your Service) platform.

Process Tracking: Explaining the lifecycle of a complaint to a user, from submission to how the Ministry verifies and closes the issue.
