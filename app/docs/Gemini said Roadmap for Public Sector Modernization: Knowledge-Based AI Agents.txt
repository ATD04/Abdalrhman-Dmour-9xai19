### Page 2: Knowledge-Based AI and Governance

* 
**Knowledge and Governance:** Government AI agents operate on approved, governed, and updated knowledge, not on open or unregulated data.


* 
**Knowledge-First Approach:** AI construction relies on managing government knowledge institutionally before deployment; data, policies, and instructions are gathered into a central Knowledge Hub with clear governance, versioning, and official approval.


* 
**Knowledge Management System:** For every piece of content, there is an official owner, a specific version, and an update history/change log.


* 
**Response Policy:** The AI agent only answers based on the latest approved version of knowledge.



---

### Page 3: Knowledge Levels within AI Agents

* 
**Layered Knowledge Model:** Each agent operates according to clear levels of guidance to ensure consistency, specialization, and commitment to government policies.


* 
**Level 1 (General Guidance):** General government policies, response style, and "do’s and don’ts" managed by government entities.


* 
**Level 2 (Sectoral Guidance):** Specialized sector information (e.g., Labor or Civil Status) managed by sectoral entities.


* 
**Level 3 (Institutional Guidance):** Specific instructions and procedures for a particular entity.


* 
**Level 4 (Departmental Guidance):** Optional specialized functions for a specific administration.



---

### Page 4: Separation Between Citizen and Employee

* 
**Access Control:** A single platform with different permissions and data based on the user category to ensure privacy and trust.


* 
**Permission Model:** Access is determined by user type (citizen/employee), job role, and government entity; agents only see authorized data.


* 
**External Agents (Citizens):** Focus on public data, announced services, service requirements, and nearest locations.


* 
**Internal Agents (Employees):** Focus on internal instructions, circulars, decisions, work procedures, and data related to their specific job roles.



---

### Page 5: Data Ingestion and Response Methodology

* 
**Operational Path:** Every response passes through a workflow ensuring accuracy, permissions, and institutional approval.


* 
**Knowledge Ingestion Path:** Approved documents are stored in the Knowledge Management System and converted into "Embeddings" (Knowledge representation).


* **Inference Flow (Response Path):**
* User query is received.


* Verification via Input Guardrail.


* Relevant knowledge retrieval (RAG) and tool utilization (MCP/Function Calling).


* Answer generation followed by Output Guardrail (accuracy and compliance).


* Final answer or transfer to a human employee.




* 
**Observability:** Continuous monitoring and evaluations (Evals).



---

### Page 6: AI Construction Methodology

* 
**Development Phases:** Starts with identifying the use case, followed by data preparation (KHJI and Embeddings), and choosing to buy or build the AI model.


* 
**Implementation:** Developing the custom agent, linking it to knowledge sources, and applying Guardrails for response control.


* 
**Deployment & Improvement:** Phased launch across digital or voice channels, followed by a continuous improvement cycle (monitoring, measuring, analyzing unanswered questions, and retraining).



---

### Page 7: Use Cases (1/3) - Knowledge Management

* 
**Unified Source:** AI-powered guidance for citizens to understand services/documents and for employees to process requests consistently.


* 
**Examples:** Specialized agents for Civil Status, Ministry of Justice, Civil Service and Administration Bureau, and Ministry of Labor.


* 
**Success Indicators:** Service efficiency (reduced time/repeated visits), higher first-contact resolution, accuracy (less contradictory info), and operational consistency.



---

### Page 8: Use Cases (2/3) - Process Mining

* 
**Process Optimization:** Analyzing digital footprints to automatically map actual workflows, identify bottlenecks, and improve service efficiency.


* **Methodology:**
* 
**Inputs:** Digital records from gov systems (time data, users, status) and standard procedures.


* 
**Processing:** Analyzing records to build actual maps, comparing execution to standards, and discovering deviations.


* 
**Outputs:** Reports on bottlenecks, critical deviations, and actionable recommendations.




* 
**Success Indicators:** Reduced cycle time, reduced delays, increased compliance with standard procedures, and operational consistency.



---

### Page 9: Use Cases (3/3) - National Call Center

* 
**Smart Gateway:** Transforming the national call center into a voice-enabled interface for handling citizen inquiries and resolving issues.


* 
**Workflow:** A "Main Agent" learns from employees and coordinates with specialized agents.


* 
**Success Indicators:** Faster resolution (reduced call time), higher first-contact resolution, service consistency, and improved accessibility for citizens with limited digital skills or disabilities.



---

### Page 10: Handling Out-of-Scope Queries

* 
**Decision Logic:** AI agents operate within defined scopes; anything outside or unclear is handled automatically.


* **Assessment Steps:**
* Is the query within the agent's scope?.


* If yes: Is the available knowledge sufficient? If yes, the agent responds.


* If no (insufficient knowledge): Transfer to a specialized human employee.


* If out of scope: Check if a specialized agent (A2A) exists to transfer the query.





---

### Page 11: Continuous Improvement Cycle

* 
**Methodology:** Agents improve through usage, measurement, and systematic knowledge updates.


* **Improvement Activities:**
* Analyzing frequently asked questions.


* Monitoring unanswered or low-quality questions.


* Updating the Knowledge Hub and retraining agents on the latest approved versions.


* Measuring answer quality, user satisfaction, response speed, and cost (via Evals).





---

### Page 12: Technology and Technical Solutions

* 
**Solution Categories:** The platform selects secure, scalable technology for knowledge drafting and reliable generation.


* 
**Foundation Models:** Enabling language understanding (e.g., OpenAI, Claude, Gemini).


* 
**Management Platforms:** For hosting and scaling agents (e.g., Nvidia NeMo, MS Foundry).


* 
**KM Tools:** Systems for storage, versioning, and approval (e.g., Document360, Pinecone).


* 
**Development Tools:** Organizing logic and task distribution (e.g., LangGraph, Pydantic AI).


* 
**Memory Tools:** Securely managing context and history (e.g., Cognee, memo.ai).


* 
**Monitoring & Analysis:** Measuring quality, cost, and errors (e.g., LangSmith, Confident AI).



---

### Page 13: Buy vs. Build Framework

* 
**Decision Framework:** Classification of use cases based on data sensitivity.


* **Public/Non-Sensitive Data:** Use ready-made APIs (OpenAI, Claude, Gemini); Low complexity, high speed, limited sovereignty.


* **Medium Sensitivity:** Use masking/hashing tools before sending data to external models; Medium complexity, balanced speed and protection.


* **High Sensitivity/Sovereign Data:** Use local LLMs (Llama, Mistral) or internal models hosted on government clouds/centers; High complexity/cost, long development time, maximum control and sovereignty.
